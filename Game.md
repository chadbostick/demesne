Fantasy Settlement Creation Game: A Guide for New Players
Introduction
The Fantasy Settlement Creation Game is a collaborative world-building experience where players take on the roles of settlers establishing a new town or outpost in a fantastical world. One player acts as the Game Master (GM), setting the stage, directing game rounds, facilitating decisions and discussions by the other players and introducing challenges, while the other players roleplay as factions within the settlement with distinct personalities and priorities. The game plays like a tabletop board game with multiple rounds and distinct phases within each round. The player designated as the Historian will chronicle the evolution of the settlement in a narrative format.
Objective
The goal of the game is to collaboratively develop a unique settlement by making strategic decisions, overcoming obstacles, and shaping the culture, economy, and governance of the new land. Each organization must work together to ensure the settlement thrives, while also competing for political power and resources within the region. Players will strive to lead the settlement through challenges, gain influence, and earn the right to name important locations such as regions, cities, and points of interest.
Culture Upgrades
In the Strategy Phase of the game, Factions will make decisions that will lead to them earning colored tokens which represent resources and political capital. In the Investment Phase, they will have the opportunity to spend those tokens to buy Culture options, which permanently changes the culture, economy or governance of the area.
Factions spend tokens to unlock Culture upgrades in one of eight categories: Politics, Property, Spirituality, Mindset, Social Order, Values, Production, and Natural Affinity.
Each Settlement starts with no Culture. Each Culture can be improved up to 3 times. At each level, the faction making the purchase has a choice in opposition. If they have enough tokens of the appropriate colors to afford that level of that Culture, they can buy one or the other option for that level. Here are the Culture Level Options:

Culture Prerequisites
✅ A faction cannot purchase a Level 2 Culture until the settlement contains a Level 1 Culture in that category.
✅ A faction cannot purchase a Level 3 Culture until the settlement contains a Level 1 and a Level 2 Culture in that category.
✅ Factions must plan their strategies with these prerequisites in mind.

All goals are achieved once the appropriate Culture is unlocked on the settlement, regardless of which faction unlocked it. A maximum score of 90 is achievable and wins the game.
Victory Points
✅ Victory Points are only awarded when a faction fully achieves a Culture Level goal.
✅ Primary Goals (Level 3 Culture) award 30 VP.
✅ Secondary Goals (Level 2 Culture) award 15 VP each. A faction has two Secondary goals and can score 30 VP total from their Secondary goals.
✅ Tertiary Goals (Level 1 Culture) award 10 VP per level unlocked. A faction can score 30 VP total for unlocking all three levels of their tertiary goal category.
✅ No partial VP is awarded for unlocking prerequisite Cultures.
Pooling Resources
✅ Factions working toward the same Culture category should consider trading or pooling tokens.
✅ A faction may gather excess tokens and trade them with allies to accelerate mutual goals.
✅ If multiple factions aim for the same Level 1, Level 2, or Level 3 Culture, they can contribute tokens together.

Roles & Responsibilities
Game Master (GM)
Describes the environment, available resources, and potential dangers.
Introduces conflicts, external factions, and natural or supernatural phenomena.
Determines consequences of player actions and facilitates story progression.
Tracks the Settlement’s Cultures, the Era, the current Phase, the game rules, and the impact on the region and wider world as the game progresses.
May introduce hidden lore or mysteries for players to uncover.
The Factions
Each player (up to six) controls a unique faction, choosing or being assigned the following characteristics:
Settler Ideologies:
Ideology: One of 16 different ideologies or worldviews, which shape their approach to settlement building. Each ideology has a unique perspective, and part of the game’s fun is debating decisions and navigating conflicts.


Progressionist – Champions innovation, social progress, and transformative change.
Goals: Democracy, Emotional, Air, Production
Conqueror – Seeks expansion and dominance over other settlements..
Goals: Empire, Tribal, Raiding, Values
Investor – Focuses on accumulating wealth and long-term economic strategy.
Goals: Banking, Rational, Trading, Social Order
Tyrant – Desires absolute control and authority over the settlement.
Goals: Taxes, Monotheism, Fire, Mindset
Empiricist – Prioritizes knowledge, logic, and evidence-based decision-making.
Goals: Science, Rational, Skill, Natural Affinity
Mystic – Seeks hidden truths, spiritual enlightenment, or divine guidance.
Goals: Mysticism, Emotional, Air, Values
Influencer – Uses charisma and networking to gain power and sway opinions.
Goals: Diplomatic, Talent, Trading, Politics
Survivalist – Focuses on self-reliance, resilience, and enduring hardships.
Goals: Isolationist, Monotheism, Monarchy, Production
Noble – Believes in aristocratic rule, tradition, and societal hierarchy.
Goals: Class, Republic, Talent, Spirituality
Champion – Aims to protect, lead, and inspire through action and heroism.
Goals: Meritocracy, Currency, Raiding, Mindset
Competitor – Thrives on rivalry, challenge, and proving superiority.
Goals: Prestige, Barter, Hierarchical, Natural Affinity
Warlord – Uses military force and intimidation to secure power.
Goals: Power, Monarchy, Polytheism, Property
Industrialist – Prioritizes progress, production, and technological advancements.
Goals: Manufacturing, Fire, Skill, Property
Exploitationist – Extracts resources and harnesses labor to maximize efficiency and power.
Goals: Mining, Barter, Hierarchical, Natural Affinity
Illuminator – Spreads knowledge, wisdom, or ideology to shape society.
Goals: Light, Republic, Tribal, Spirituality
Deceiver – Manipulates, deceives, and uses subterfuge to control outcomes.
Goals: Dark, Currency, Polytheism, Politics
(Other, Created by the player) - Something unique that best describes the player's vision for their organization.
Species: A humanoid or sapient species from D&D 5e, representing the leadership, dominant culture, or most populous group within their organization.


Organization Type: Defines how the faction is structured, such as a guild, family, church, survivors, explorers, researchers, or another collective.


Organization Name: Players create a name for their group, adding flavor to their settlement’s identity.


Goals: Each faction has three goals at the beginning of the game or when they are added. If the player creates a custom Ideology, they must also create a primary, secondary and tertiary goal:


Primary Goal: A specific Level 3 Culture Option (worth 30 Victory Points).
Secondary Goal: Two specific** Level 2 Culture Options** (worth 15** Victory Points** each).
Tertiary Goal: A Culture category different from both the Primary and Secondary Goals (worth 10 Victory Points per level unlocked).
Tokens: Each faction starts with 0 tokens.


Victory Points (VP): Each faction starts with 0 VP.


Each player is responsible for tracking their tokens, victory points, progress toward goals, and their choices during each game phase.
Leading Faction
At the beginning of the game, the GM determines which faction is the Leading Faction. Regardless of whether the settlement has a monarch, a governing body, or is completely anarchical, the Leading Faction represents those making decisions for the community during the Challenge Phase and has the ability to spend tokens to remove a Culture option from the settlement.

Game Structure
The game is divided into Eras, each consisting of four phases:
1. Strategy Phase
Each faction determines how they will grow their influence while helping the settlement thrive. They pick one of the available Strategies and describe how their faction implements it.
At the beginning of the game, six Strategies are available:
Pray (Provides red tokens)
Discuss (Provides blue tokens)
Lead (Provides green tokens)
Organize (Provides orange tokens)
Forage (Provides pink tokens)
Make (Exchange one token for two others; see below)
The GM rolls a d20 for each of the first 5 Strategies and determines how many tokens are provided.
1-4 = 0 tokens
5-15 = 1 token
16-19 = 2 tokens
20 = 2 tokens of the appropriate color plus 1 additonal token of the Faction's choice
When a faction chooses the Make strategy, they can pick one of their colored tokens and exchange it for two others of a different color. At the beginning of the game, there are five Make options:
Holy Site (Exchange 1 Red for 2 tokens)
Commons (Exchange 1 Blue for 2 tokens)
Marker (Exchange 1 Green for 2 tokens)
Storehouse (Exchange 1 Orange for 2 tokens)
Workyard (Exchange 1 Pink for 2 tokens)
✅ Make is ideal when a faction needs multiple token types for a Culture upgrade.
✅ Make should be used to convert excess resources into necessary ones.
✅ A faction can use Make to create tradeable tokens for future exchanges.
✅ If a faction needs two different tokens (e.g., 1 Red + 1 Blue), Make can instantly provide both.
Unlocking Cultures can introduce new Strategies and Make options. The faction that purchased the Culture decides the name of the new Strategy and Make option, and all factions can use them going forward.
Spirituality unlocks a new red Strategy and red Make.
Personality unlocks a new blue Strategy and blue Make.
Society unlocks a new blue Strategy and blue Make.
Values unlock a new green Strategy and green Make.
Politics unlocks a new orange Strategy and orange Make.
Property unlocks a new orange Strategy and orange Make.
Production  unlocks a new pink Strategy and pink Make.
Elements unlocks a new pink Strategy and pink Make.
Depending on the level of the Culture option unlocked, the payouts for the new Strategy and Make will change.
Level 1 Culture's Strategy Payout
1 = 0 tokens
2-4 = 1 token
5-16 = 2 tokens
17-19 = 3 tokens
20 = 3 tokens of the appropriate color plus 1 additional token of the Faction's choice
Level 1 Culture's Make
Exchange 2 tokens of the appropriate color for 4 tokens of any color(s)
Level 2 Culture's Strategy
1 = 1 token
2-3 = 2 tokens
4-9 = 3 tokens
10-15 = 4 tokens
16-19 = 5 tokens of the appropriate color plus 2 additional tokens of the Faction's choice
20 = 6 tokens of the appropriate color plus 3 additional tokens of the Faction's choice
Level 2 Culture's Make
Exchange 3 tokens of the appropriate color for 6 tokens of any color(s)
Level 3 Culture's Strategy
1-4 = 2 tokens
5-10 = 4 tokens
11-15 = 6 tokens
16-19 = 6 tokens of the appropriate color plus 4 tokens of any combination of colors
20 = 12 tokens of any color combination
Level 3 Culture's Make
Exchange 4 tokens of the appropriate color for 10 tokens of any color(s)
✅ When a faction uses Make, they must describe what they are crafting and its settlement-wide impact.
✅ Make creations can be religious symbols, military installations, technological advancements, or cultural landmarks.
✅ The GM can integrate Make creations into future Challenges and story developments.
✅ Factions should align their Make creations with their ideology and goals.
2. Investments Phase
Factions decide whether to pursue their goals independently or collaborate by pooling resources with other players. Some investments may help achieve long-term goals, even if they don’t immediately grant Victory Points.
If a player reaches 80 points, they immediately win the game. Otherwise, the game continues for 10 Eras unless the GM determines the settlement has failed or reached its peak.
Factions spend tokens to unlock Culture upgrades in one of eight categories:
Politics
Property
Spirituality
Personality
Society
Values
Production
Elements
Factions may pool tokens to unlock a Culture Level. The purchasing faction chooses one of two opposing options per level. If a Culture contradicts the Leading Faction’s ideology, they may spend tokens to remove it at the original cost.
3. Challenge Phase
The Leading Faction faces a settlement-wide challenge and decides how to respond, spending tokens as necessary. The Factions can donate as many tokens as they feel necessary to overcome the challenge.
Other factions may donate tokens if they support the Leading Faction’s decision.


The GM rolls a d20 to determine success and adds +1 to the result for each token donated by the Leading Faction and/or other Factions. A total of 10 or more is a success and 9 or fewer is a failure.


Success: The Leading Faction stays in power, and the settlement receives a Boon.


Failure: The challenge negatively impacts the settlement, the Leading Faction loses power, and the GM assigns a new Leading Faction.


✅ Tokens spent to resolve a Challenge are permanently removed from the faction’s pool.
✅ Before entering the Strategy Phase, factions must reassess their available tokens.
✅ If a faction spends a large number of tokens, they should adjust their next strategy to recover.
4. End of an Era Phase
The GM summarizes the settlement’s progress, challenges, and faction power shifts. Players reflect on their points and goals, considering their next moves before the game advances to the next Era.
